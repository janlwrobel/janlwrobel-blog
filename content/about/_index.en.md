# About Me

Hi, I’m Jan.

I work in IT support in the UK, dealing with the kind of problems that never appear in Microsoft marketing screenshots.

Outlook stops syncing five minutes before meetings.  
OneDrive gets stuck on “Looking for changes”.  
Printers refuse to print for no reason.  
Teams worked perfectly yesterday - today it suddenly doesn’t.

This blog is where I document real-world IT troubleshooting, homelab projects, and lessons learned from working on the front line of support.

Before moving into IT, I worked in manufacturing, mechanical assembly, and quality control. I also studied Mechanical Engineering at the Koszalin University of Technology in Poland.

That background taught me something I still use every day:
how to troubleshoot problems logically under pressure.

Outside work, I build a homelab based on Proxmox, experiment with networking, automation, Docker, Cloudflare Tunnel, and self-hosted services, and constantly break things so I can learn how to fix them properly.

This blog is not about theory.

It’s about what actually breaks in IT - and how to fix it.
